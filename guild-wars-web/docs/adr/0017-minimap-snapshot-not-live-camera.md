The Walk Mode minimap is built from a static top-down snapshot of a small area (10-unit radius) around the entry point, captured once as Walk Mode is entered, rather than a live second 3D camera rendered every frame or a single bitmap covering the whole world.

The "not a live camera" half was chosen because Walk Mode already renders the full scene once per frame with no dirty-check (`animate()` returns early for walk mode, skipping the iso camera's `camMoved`/`needsRender` guard), making it the worst place to add a second unconditional full-scene render. A snapshot decouples the expensive part (a full top-down render) from the cheap part (cropping and rotating a bitmap to follow the player).

The "local, not whole-world" half was chosen after the first cut (a single bitmap covering the full `[0, worldSize]²` world, refreshed on every geometry change) turned out to fight the minimap's tight zoom: matching the full world at a resolution sharp enough for a ~1-2 world-unit crop would need a very large bitmap. Capturing only a small radius around the entry point gets far more detail per world-unit at a much smaller bitmap size, and is captured fresh on every Walk Mode entry — so it's also simpler, with no "is this stale, do I need to recapture" bookkeeping tied to geometry-change events. The trade-off: if the player wanders past the captured radius, the minimap shows blank past that edge until they exit and re-enter Walk Mode. Accepted, since Walk Mode sessions are typically local exploration near the entry point.
